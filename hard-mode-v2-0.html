How to Play

Objective: Tune the second tone to match the base frequency by adjusting its pitch up (↑) or down (↓) using the vertical controls. Minimize the frequency differential with the fewest moves and shortest time.

Gameplay:

  • Start Game: Click "Start Game" to begin. Two tones will play: a base tone and a second tone to adjust.
  • Adjust Pitch: Click any of the 8 buttons in the vertical stack. Green ↑ buttons raise the pitch, red ↓ buttons lower it. Each move applies a random adjustment within the size range:
    • Big: 3.01–6.00 Hz (darkest)
    • Medium: 1.01–3.00 Hz
    • Small: 0.51–1.00 Hz
    • Tiny: 0.00–0.50 Hz (lightest)
  • All Available: Every button works from the first move—no separate phases.
  • Listen: The beating slows as tones match (differential nears 0 Hz).
  • Finish: Click "Finished" when you believe the tones match. Score calculated.
  • Reset: Click "Reset / Stop Tones" to stop tones and reset.

Scoring: (Moves + Time in Seconds) × Frequency Differential (Hz). Lower scores better—fewer moves, less time, smaller differential.

Official Score: Average of your last 5 scores (after 5+ attempts).

Modes: "Tuning Two Tenors" (similar pitches), "Tuning Tenor to Bass," or "Tuning Tenor to Chanter" (octave down).
